Conditions

Common Mental Health Conditions

Mental health conditions are more common than most people realize. Understanding them reduces stigma and helps people seek help sooner.

9 Key Conditions

Recognized Mental Health Conditions

This is not an exhaustive list — there are hundreds of recognized mental health conditions. These are among the most prevalent worldwide.

Mood
Depression

Persistent sadness, loss of interest, fatigue, and difficulty functioning. Affects 280 million people globally and is a leading cause of disability worldwide.

Anxiety
Anxiety Disorders

Excessive worry, fear, or panic that interferes with daily life. Includes generalized anxiety disorder, social anxiety, panic disorder, and specific phobias.

Mood
Bipolar Disorder

Episodes of extreme mood swings — from manic highs to depressive lows — affecting sleep, energy, behavior, judgment, and ability to carry out daily tasks.

Trauma
PTSD

Post-traumatic stress disorder develops after exposure to a traumatic event, causing flashbacks, nightmares, severe anxiety, and uncontrollable intrusive thoughts.

Obsessive
OCD

Obsessive-compulsive disorder involves recurring, unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions) that feel impossible to control.

Psychosis
Schizophrenia

A serious disorder affecting thinking, feelings, and behavior. People may experience hallucinations, delusions, and severely disordered thinking patterns.

Behavioral
Eating Disorders

Conditions like anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge-eating disorder involve severely distorted relationships with food, weight, and body image.

Neurodevelopmental
ADHD

Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder affects focus, impulse control, and energy regulation. Common in children and often continues into adulthood.

Personality
Borderline Personality

Characterized by intense emotional swings, unstable relationships, impulsive behavior, and a persistent unstable sense of self and identity.
